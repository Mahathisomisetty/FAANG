India is a land of rich history and culture, boasting numerous iconic monuments. Among them, the Taj Mahal, India Gate, Charminar, and Thanjavur (Brihadeeswarar Temple) stand out as prominent examples. The Taj Mahal, an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of love. India Gate, a war memorial in Delhi, is a national monument and a popular gathering place. Charminar, a historic mosque and minaret in Hyderabad, is an iconic symbol of the city. Thanjavur, in Tamil Nadu, is home to the magnificent Brihadeeswarar Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site and a prime example of Chola architecture.

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India, commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It's renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate details, and as a symbol of love.

Charminar, historic monument located at the heart of Hyderabad, west-central Telangana state, south-central India. The city, which is the capital of Telangana state, was also the capital of the historic princely state of Hyderabad.

Brihadishvara Temple, called Rajarajesvaram by its builder, and known locally as Thanjai Periya Kovil and Peruvudaiyar Kovil, is a Shaivite Hindu temple built in a Chola architectural style located on the south bank of the Cauvery river in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, India

India Gate, monumental sandstone arch in New Delhi, dedicated to the troops of British India who died in wars fought between 1914 and 1919. India Gate, which is located at the eastern end of the Rajpath (formerly called the Kingsway), is about 138 feet (42 metres) in height.
